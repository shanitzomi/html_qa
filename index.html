Living in Israel's south, a citizen of the Negev,
leaves me wondering when will the HiTech companies finally join us.
I see it as just a matter of time.


Tel Aviv, currently the center of HiTech in Israel, the most expensive and crowded city in the country.
A city with inadequate parking and heavy time-consuming traffic sharpen the need for relocation,
expansion and the creation of new centers around the country primarily in the North and South of the country.


The benefits of living and working in the periphery (lower costs of living,
less traffic, tax benefits and above all a better quality of life) are well known.
Encouraging HiTech companies to establish themselves in these areas will create a stronger workforce and strengthen the area.
Recently a few companies started to make this move such as Intel in Beer Sheva, HiTech Parks in Ashkelon and also in the North. Its only the beginning.

The OR Movement, an organization which encourages growth in the south and north of the country,
has taken upon itself to build 3 centers around the country by 2048.


That's a vision!

I do have a vision that the North and the South of Israel
will become flourishing centers of HiTech thus increasing the quality of life here through higher work opportunities for the current and future residents.
